POWER, FREEDOM & SOCIETY

How Should Society Be Structured?

Political philosophy examines authority, rights, and freedom.

John Locke — Natural Rights

Locke argued that all humans possess rights to life, liberty, and property.

Karl Marx — Class and Capital

Marx believed economic systems shape society and argued for radical restructuring.

Jean-Paul Sartre — Radical Freedom

Sartre believed humans are free and fully responsible for their choices.

Simone de Beauvoir — Social Oppression

She explored how society constructs identity and limits freedom.

Final Reflection

Philosophy is not about memorizing names. It is about learning to question, reason, and build a coherent view of the world. The ideas of these thinkers continue to shape technology, politics, ethics, and personal identity today.
