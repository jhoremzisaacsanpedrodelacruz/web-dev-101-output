THE QUESTION OF MORALITY

How Should We Live?

Ethics explores right and wrong, justice, and responsibility.

Aristotle — Virtue Ethics

Aristotle believed morality is about developing good character. A good life is achieved through balance and habit.

Immanuel Kant — Duty

Kant argued that morality is based on universal principles. Actions are right if they can be applied universally.

John Stuart Mill — Utilitarianism

Mill believed actions are right if they maximize happiness for the greatest number of people.

Friedrich Nietzsche — Critique of Morality

Nietzsche questioned traditional moral systems and argued that individuals must create their own values.

Modern Impact

Law, human rights, medicine, and political systems all depend on ethical philosophy.
