TIMELINE OF PHILOSOPHY

Ancient Foundations

The birth of philosophy in ancient Greece marked humanity's first systematic attempt to understand reality through reason rather than myth. From 600 BCE onwards, thinkers like Thales, Heraclitus, and Pythagoras asked fundamental questions about the nature of matter, change, and order. Socrates revolutionized philosophy by focusing on ethical inquiry and the examination of life itself. Plato created a comprehensive philosophical system addressing reality, knowledge, ethics, and politics. Aristotle brought empirical observation to philosophy and created frameworks for logic, metaphysics, ethics, and politics that would dominate Western thought for centuries.

The Enlightenment

The seventeenth and eighteenth centuries saw philosophy transform the intellectual landscape of Europe and beyond. Descartes questioned all received wisdom and sought absolute certainty through reason alone. Locke and Hume explored how knowledge arises from sensory experience. Kant unified rationalism and empiricism, arguing that the mind actively shapes our experience of reality. Smith examined economics and moral sentiment. These thinkers laid the groundwork for modern science, democracy, and economic theory, fundamentally reshaping how humans understood themselves and society.

Modern Thought

From the nineteenth century to today, philosophy has become increasingly diverse, pluralistic, and global. Hegel explored history as the development of human freedom. Marx examined capitalism and class. Nietzsche challenged fundamental values and morality itself. Pragmatists asked what difference ideas make in practice. Analytic philosophers developed formal logic and language philosophy. Continental thinkers explored consciousness, existence, and power. Feminist philosophers examined how gender shapes knowledge and society. Contemporary philosophy engages with artificial intelligence, environmental ethics, and global justice, proving that philosophy remains vital for understanding our rapidly changing world.
